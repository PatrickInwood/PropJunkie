### DAY 4 — First methodology piece: "How the model builds a projection"

**X thread (the Data-Nerd magnet). Needs graphic: optional simple diagram card.**
> 1/ How does PropJunkie actually pick a side? No "gut," no insider info. Here's the whole method — it's simpler and more humble than you'd think. 🧵
>
> 2/ For each team we estimate expected runs/points from recent offense vs. the opponent's recent defense, scaled to league average. Log5-style. Basically: how good have you been, adjusted for who you're facing.
>
> 3/ Then we regress those rates toward the league average. Small samples lie. A team isn't as hot (or as cold) as its last 5 games say. Pulling toward the mean keeps the model from overreacting.
>
> 4/ For MLB specifically we blend in the probable STARTING PITCHERS' ERA — the single biggest factor in one game. A 2.10-ERA arm vs a 5.40-ERA arm moves the total more than almost anything else.
>
> 5/ From projected scores we derive a Pythagorean win probability, then compare it to the market line. The market is a strong prior — if we wildly disagree, we assume WE'RE missing info, not them.
>
> 6/ That's why it's a lean, not a lock. It's built to be honest about uncertainty, not to sound confident. And every lean it produces gets graded at propjunkie.app/record.
>
> 18+. For entertainment. Gamble responsibly.
