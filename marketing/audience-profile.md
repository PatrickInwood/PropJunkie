# PropJunkie — Audience Profile & Content Strategy

**Niche:** Sports betting picks-analyzer / model-based handicapping
**Prepared for:** Patrick (owner, PropJunkie — propjunkie.app)
**Strategic wedge:** Honest, transparent, "a lean not a lock — and we show our record." PropJunkie is 100% free and publicly grades its own model at `/record`. That transparency is the entire angle against a niche built on hype.

> **Compliance baseline (applies to every post built from this doc):** For informational & entertainment purposes only. Must be 18+ (21+ where required) and in a jurisdiction where sports betting is legal. Please gamble responsibly. No guarantees, no "locks," no invented win rates — only real leans and the real, tracked `/record` numbers.

---

## 0. The strategic reading (start here)

This niche is loud, crowded, and low-trust. Almost every competitor sells the same thing: confidence they can't back up ("🔒 LOCK OF THE DAY," fake screenshots, deleted losing bets). The audience has been burned repeatedly and *knows* it. That is the opening.

PropJunkie does not out-hype the touts — it does the opposite. The brand personality is **the sharp, skeptical friend who shows his math and admits when he's wrong.** Every feature maps to a specific wound the touts have left open:

| Audience wound | The tout norm | PropJunkie's answer |
|---|---|---|
| "They only show their wins." | Cherry-picked screenshots | **`/record`** — every lean graded vs. the final score, publicly |
| "It's a $200/month Discord." | Paywalls, upsells | **100% free**, no paywall |
| "They sound way too sure." | "GUARANTEED LOCK" | **Model leans** — "lean not a lock," market-anchored, humble by design |
| "I don't understand *why* the pick." | "Trust me bro" | **Prop Analyzer** + transparent model logic (recent form, MLB pitcher ERA) |
| "Where's the data from — is it even real?" | Vague | **Free public sources** (ESPN, MLB statsapi), stated openly |

The content job is not to convince people betting is easy. It's to convince people that *this one* is honest — and honesty, in this niche, is a genuinely differentiated product.

## 1. The most profitable audience segments

Ranked by strategic value to a free, transparency-led product. "Profitable" here = most likely to follow, stick, evangelize, and (later) convert to any premium tier — not people to extract money from.

### Segment A — "The Burned Skeptic" (PRIMARY target)
- **Who:** 25–45, has bought at least one tout's picks or joined a paid Discord and got burned. Bets recreationally, moderate stakes. Follows betting content but with arms crossed. Financially literate enough to be suspicious.
- **Why they're the #1 target:** They are *actively looking for someone honest* and have money and attention to give — but only to a brand that earns trust first. PropJunkie's entire identity is built for them. They convert slowly but become the loudest advocates once won.
- **What wins them:** The `/record` page, "here's a pick that LOST and why," transparent methodology. Proof over promises.
- **Differs from others:** Lowest tolerance for hype, highest lifetime loyalty once earned.

### Segment B — "The Data/Model Nerd" (PRIMARY target)
- **Who:** 22–40, often works or studies in tech, finance, analytics, or is a fantasy-sports veteran. Enjoys the *process* — EV, closing line value, sample size — as much as the outcome. May bet small or barely bet; loves the intellectual game.
- **Why worth it:** High engagement, high share rate, and they scrutinize your methodology publicly — which, if you're honest, becomes free credibility. They screenshot good data content into group chats. Cheap to reach, huge reach multiplier.
- **What wins them:** Methodology threads, the MLB starting-pitcher-ERA factor, "why the model regresses to the mean," model-vs-market disagreement breakdowns, accuracy stats by sport/market.
- **Differs from others:** They'll forgive a losing week if the *process* is sound. They punish overconfidence hardest.

### Segment C — "The Casual Slate-Checker" (VOLUME target)
- **Who:** 21–40, bets a few games a week — mostly the big matchups, primetime, their home team. Not trying to "beat the market," just wants a smarter, quicker gut-check before they tap a bet. Largest population by far.
- **Why worth it:** Volume and top-of-funnel. They're the audience that makes a free daily-slate product a daily habit. Low individual value, enormous aggregate reach, and the pool your advocates come from.
- **What wins them:** The **Daily Slate** as a fast daily ritual, "⭐ model leans" on tonight's games, clean matchup cards, one-glance value.
- **Differs from others:** Won't read a methodology thread. Wants the answer, fast, free, and repeatable.

### Segment D — "The Prop Specialist" (NICHE-FIT target)
- **Who:** 24–45, focuses on player props over sides/totals. Follows player news, injuries, minutes/usage. Often more disciplined than the average bettor.
- **Why worth it:** The **Prop Analyzer** is purpose-built for them, and props are the fastest-growing corner of the market. Smaller segment but a direct feature-to-need match = high conversion to habitual use.
- **What wins them:** Prop Analyzer breakdowns — "here's the projection from his last N games vs. the line," recent-form visuals.
- **Differs from others:** Player-centric, not game-centric. Lives on news timelines.

**De-prioritize (do not chase):** the "get-rich-quick / parlay-degen" crowd chasing 15-leg lottery tickets. They want the fantasy PropJunkie deliberately refuses to sell, they churn instantly, and courting them corrodes the honest brand and the compliance posture. Serve responsibly or not at all.

## 2. Their biggest frustrations

**With touts / pick services:**
1. **Survivorship theater** — only winners get posted; losers quietly deleted. → *Directly answered by `/record`.*
2. **Paywalls with no proof** — $50–$300/month before you can see if they're any good. → *Answered by being 100% free.*
3. **Manufactured certainty** — "🔒 GUARANTEED LOCK" on a coin-flip game. Insults their intelligence. → *Answered by "lean not a lock."*
4. **No reasoning** — a pick with zero "why." Feels like a random number generator. → *Answered by transparent model + Prop Analyzer.*
5. **Fake track records** — invented "72% win rate" with no receipts. → *Answered by a self-grading, public record.*

**With betting generally:**
6. **Information overload** — too many stats, too little time before lock. *(Slate = fast synthesis.)*
7. **Recency bias / tilt** — chasing losses, overreacting to one game.
8. **Feeling like the sucker** — the sense that the book always knows more. *(Market-anchored model respects that instead of pretending to beat it every night.)*
9. **Distrust of "where's this even from?"** — opaque data. *(Free public sources, stated.)*

**Strategic note:** frustrations 1–5 are exactly the five wounds in Section 0. Content should *name the frustration out loud* before presenting the feature. Agitate the shared enemy (dishonest touts), then position PropJunkie as the alternative — never by mocking a real named competitor, just the archetype.

## 3. Emotional triggers

What actually moves this audience (use these as the emotional spine of posts):

- **Vindication / "I knew it."** They suspect the touts are frauds. Content that validates that suspicion ("here's why 'lock of the day' is marketing, not math") feels *true* and gets shared.
- **Belonging to the smart minority.** Nobody wants to be the mark. "Bet like you read the fine print" makes them feel like the sharp one in the group chat.
- **Trust through vulnerability.** Counterintuitive but powerful: *showing a loss* builds more trust than showing a win. It signals you're not hiding anything. This is PropJunkie's superpower — lean into losing-pick transparency.
- **Curiosity / the "why."** "The model likes the under here — and it's the starting pitcher's ERA doing the work." Satisfying-reveal energy.
- **FOMO, handled honestly.** The daily slate creates a healthy "check before tonight's games" ritual — urgency without hype.
- **Control / competence.** They want to feel like a disciplined analyst, not a gambler. Frame the product as a *tool for thinking*, not a shortcut to riches.

**Avoid these triggers (compliance + brand):** greed ("turn $10 into $10k"), desperation/loss-chasing, and any implication that betting is income. These attract the wrong segment and cross the lines in your guardrails.

## 4. Content consumption habits

**When:** Betting content spikes on a daily game-clock rhythm — late afternoon into evening as people build tonight's card, and a second spike right before primetime tip/first pitch. Weekends and big event days (opening weeks, playoffs) are peaks. Post the slate/leans content in the **pre-game window** (roughly midday to a couple hours before first games) so it's actionable, and recaps/record content the **morning after**.

**Where & how, by platform:**
- **X/Twitter** — the live nerve center of sports betting. This is where Segments A (Skeptic) and B (Data Nerd) live. Real-time, text + chart, thread-friendly, screenshot-driven. Highest-trust format for methodology and receipts.
- **Instagram** — visual, slower, more casual. Segment C (Casual) and D (Props). Carousels for "teach me something" content, clean graphic cards for daily leans, Stories for the daily ritual and quick polls.
- **TikTok** — discovery engine, youngest skew (still 21+ only — never target minors). Segments C and D, plus new-to-betting curious viewers. Short, fast, face-to-camera or screen-capture. Personality and "here's a thing you didn't know" formats travel furthest.

**Formats they prefer:**
- Receipts (screenshots of the actual `/record`, graded picks).
- Short methodology explainers (chart or 30–60s clip).
- Clean, glanceable daily cards (matchup + lean + honest edge + disclaimer).
- "Hot take that's actually backed by data."
- Contrarian myth-busting about the betting industry itself.

**What they scroll past:** walls of text with no visual, obvious hype, anything that smells like a paid-Discord funnel, picks with no reasoning.

## 5. Follow → Engage → Convert (with concrete post types)

For a free product, "convert" = **become a daily active user of the Slate/Analyzer, and share it.** Monetization comes later; trust and habit come first.

### Stage 1 — FOLLOW ("this account is different / worth it")
Goal: signal honesty + competence in the first impression.
- **"We grade every pick — win or lose" intro post/pin.** Screenshot of `/record`. The thesis statement of the whole brand. *(Pin this on X and IG.)*
- **Industry myth-bust:** "Why 'lock of the day' is a marketing phrase, not a math one." Names the shared enemy.
- **A losing pick, owned:** "The model was wrong here last night. Here's the game and what missed." Nothing earns a follow in this niche faster than visible honesty.
- **Free-and-transparent flex:** "No paywall. No Discord upsell. The full slate and our record are free — here's the link."

### Stage 2 — ENGAGE (replies, shares, saves, polls)
Goal: reward participation, build the "smart minority" community.
- **Model vs. You:** post the model's lean, ask "agree or fade?" Poll on IG Stories / quote-prompt on X. Fans love disagreeing with a model.
- **Methodology thread (X) / carousel (IG):** "How our model builds a projection" — recent form, regression to the mean, and for MLB the starting-pitcher ERA factor. The Data Nerds share these.
- **Prop Analyzer breakdown:** "Player X's last 10 games vs. tonight's line — here's the projection." Save-bait for prop bettors.
- **"Guess the record" pre-reveal:** ask followers to guess this week's model hit-rate before showing the real `/record` number.
- **Recap the honest way:** "Model went [real W-L] on last night's leans. Here's the one it nailed and the one it missed." Real numbers only.

### Stage 3 — CONVERT (visit, use daily, share the tool)
Goal: turn a follower into a habitual user + word-of-mouth.
- **Daily Slate CTA card:** "Tonight's ⭐ model leans are live. Free. [link]" — the workhorse daily post, always with the short disclaimer.
- **Feature spotlight:** a 30–60s TikTok/Reel screen-walk of the Prop Analyzer or the Slate tabs — "here's the actual tool, free, no signup wall."
- **Record milestone (only with real data):** when `/record` has a genuine sample, "The model's tracked record so far: [real number from /record]. We show all of it." *Never* fabricate this — if the sample is thin, sell the *transparency* ("we grade every one publicly"), not a stat.
- **The "check it before you bet" ritual:** frame the Slate as the last stop before you tap a bet — a habit, not a hard sell.

**Conversion principle:** the CTA is *"see for yourself, it's free"* — never *"trust me."* The product's openness does the selling; the post just opens the door.

## 6. Platform playbook + first-30-days cold start

Three accounts, zero followers, not posted yet. Priorities and a cold-start angle for each.

### X/Twitter — **primary account. Where trust is won.**
- **Lives here:** Burned Skeptics (A) + Data Nerds (B).
- **Formats:** receipts, methodology threads, quick reaction takes, `/record` screenshots.
- **First 30 days:** Build *credibility density* before reach. (1) Pin the "we grade every pick" post. (2) Post the daily slate lean(s) every day in the pre-game window — consistency signals a real operation. (3) One methodology thread per week. (4) Reply thoughtfully to bigger betting-X accounts and mid-size handicappers with genuine data points (not spam links) — this is the main discovery lever with zero followers. (5) Every few days, when a lean loses, own it. Goal by day 30: a small but visibly *honest* body of work anyone can scroll and trust.

### Instagram — **the polished shop window.**
- **Lives here:** Casual Slate-Checkers (C) + Prop Specialists (D).
- **Formats:** branded daily-lean cards (feed), teaching carousels, Stories for the daily ritual + polls.
- **First 30 days:** Establish visual identity and rhythm. (1) 3 feed posts/week minimum — one daily-lean card format, one "here's a feature" card, one myth-bust/teaching carousel. (2) Daily Story: "tonight's slate is live" + an agree/fade poll. (3) Use the brand kit consistently (gold "Prop" / light "Junkie", dark premium look) so the grid reads as one confident brand from post #1. Cold-start reach comes from Reels (see below cross-post) and hashtags; the feed is what converts a profile-visit into a follow.

### TikTok — **the discovery engine / top of funnel.**
- **Lives here:** Casuals (C), Prop fans (D), betting-curious 21+.
- **Formats:** 20–45s face-to-camera or screen-capture. Personality + one insight per video.
- **First 30 days:** Volume + a repeatable series. (1) Post 4–5x/week — TikTok rewards frequency for new accounts more than any other platform. (2) Run one signature series, e.g. **"Grading our own picks"** — a recurring format where you show yesterday's leans and the real result, win or lose. It's native, honest, and impossible for touts to copy. (3) A second series: **"One thing the books don't want casuals to know"** — myth-busts. (4) Cross-post the best TikToks as IG Reels to feed the IG cold start. Hook in the first 2 seconds; disclaimer on-screen for anything pick-related.

**Cross-platform cadence summary (starter):**

| | X | Instagram | TikTok |
|---|---|---|---|
| Posts/week | 7+ (daily leans) + 1 thread | 3 feed + daily Stories | 4–5 |
| Hero format | Receipts + methodology | Daily-lean cards + carousels | "Grading our own picks" series |
| Cold-start lever | Reply-in-public to betting accounts | Reels + consistent branded grid | Post frequency + signature series |
| Primary segment | Skeptic + Data Nerd | Casual + Props | Casual + curious |

## 7. Guardrails (never ship a post that breaks these)

1. **Responsible-gambling disclaimer on anything bet-related.** Full: *"For informational & entertainment purposes only. Must be 18+ (21+ where required) and in a jurisdiction where sports betting is legal. Please gamble responsibly."* Short (tight platforms): *"21+. For entertainment. Gamble responsibly."*
2. **No guarantees.** No "lock," "guaranteed," "can't lose," "free money," or invented win rates.
3. **Only real numbers.** Any hit-rate or pick cites real `/record` data or a real lean. No data? Sell the transparency, not a stat.
4. **No targeting minors or problem gamblers**, and never imply betting is a path to income.
